# BBA Four-Year Degree Map

A representative Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) sequence, synthesized
from the public core curricula of NYU Stern, BU Questrom, Emory Goizueta,
UMN Carlson, Fordham Gabelli, and Rutgers Business School. These schools
converge on the same functional core; the numbering below is our own,
organized so the first digit = year, so `203.4` reads as
"a Year-2 course, course #3 in that year (the `0`), topic #4."

> Course code scheme: `YNN.T`
> - `Y`  = academic year (1–4)
> - `NN` = course slot within the year (01–08; semester 1 = 01–04, semester 2 = 05–08)
> - `T`  = topic/lesson number within that course
>
> Example: `105.2` = Year 1, course slot 05 (Sem 2, course A), topic 2.
> When you say "It's time to work on 203.4 Supply and Demand," I resolve
> `2` → Year 2, `03` → third course slot, `4` → fourth topic in its outline.

This project only builds **Year 1** outlines in full (see `01-year-1.md`).
Years 2–4 are mapped at the course level here so you can see the arc and
extend later.

## Year 1 — Foundations (detailed outlines in `01-year-1.md`)

**Semester 1**
- `101` Financial Accounting I — the language of business
- `102` Microeconomics — individual decisions, firms, and markets
- `103` Business Statistics & Data Literacy I
- `104` Foundations of Management & Organizational Behavior

**Semester 2**
- `105` Financial Accounting II / Intro to Managerial Accounting
- `106` Macroeconomics — aggregate economy and policy
- `107` Marketing Principles
- `108` Business Communication & Professional Skills

---

## Year 2 — Business Essentials & Analytics

**Semester 1**
- `201` Managerial / Cost Accounting
- `202` Corporate Finance I (time value, valuation basics)
- `203` Intermediate Microeconomics (Supply & Demand deep dive lives here)
- `204` Business Statistics II / Inferential Methods

**Semester 2**
- `205` Information Systems & Digital Transformation
- `206` Operations & Supply Chain Management
- `207` Organizational Behavior & Leading Teams
- `208` Business Law, Contracts & Regulation

---

## Year 3 — Integration & Decision-Making

**Semester 1**
- `301` Corporate Finance II / Investments
- `302` Marketing Analytics & Consumer Behavior
- `303` Data-Driven Decision Analytics (modeling, optimization)
- `304` Business Ethics & Corporate Responsibility

**Semester 2**
- `305` Human Capital Management
- `306` International / Global Business
- `307` Management Information Systems & Emerging Tech

## Notes on synthesis

- Every school in the sample front-loads **accounting, micro/macroeconomics,
  statistics, and an intro management or communications course** in Year 1.
  That is the consensus first-year load reflected above.
- "I-Core" / integrated-core schools (Fordham, Carlson) cluster
  marketing + finance + operations + strategy in Year 2–3; we preserve that arc.
- The Year-1 outlines deliberately match what a first-year student actually
  takes, since that was the explicit request. Later years are intentionally
  lighter (course-level only) and meant to be expanded with the same
  outline format when you reach them.
